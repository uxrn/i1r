Frequently Asked Questions

What are Handshake domains?

Handshake domains are decentralized top-level domains (dTLDs) that operate independently of ICANN. They use blockchain technology to provide censorship-resistant domain names that you fully control.

How do I make a Handshake website?

You can make a Handshake website the same way you make a normal website. For simple, static websites, we recommend using GitHub Pages as a free host. Once you make your website and upload it to a host, you need to configure your Handshake domain's DNS records to point to your website.

How do I access my Handshake website?

You can access Handshake domains by configuring your DNS resolver to use a Handshake-compatible resolver like NextDNS, HDNS, or HNS DoH. These resolvers allow you to browse Handshake domains directly in your standard web browsers, such as Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari, etc.

At first, your Handshake domain will only be accessible via http://. In order to use https://, additional configuration is required.

Alternatively, if you purchase a .dn, .rd, or .ur domain, you can simply append .hn.sb or .hns.to to the end of the domain name (e.g. test.dn.hn.sb) to access it immediately through all standard DNS resolvers and web browsers (no additional software or configuration required).

Note: The hn.sb resolver is offered by i1Registry for testing purposes only and should not be used in production environments.

What's the difference between Handshake, Unstoppable Domains, and Freename?

The Handshake blockchain offers truly decentralized top-level domains (dTLDs) that are not subject to the control of any central authority. Unstoppable Domains and Freename, on the other hand, are centralized domain name services that are subject to the control of their respective parent companies.

With Handshake domains, you have full control and there is no central point of failure (i.e. an actual blockchain). With Unstoppable and Freename domains, you are at the mercy of the parent company. If they cease to exist, so do your domains through them.

Can I use Handshake domains for cryptocurrency wallets?

Yes! Handshake domains can be used as human-readable names for cryptocurrency addresses. This allows you to send and receive crypto and NFTs using your domain name instead of long, complex wallet addresses.
